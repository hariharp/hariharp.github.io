Designing Teradata's Campus Certification Initiative


How prioritizing student needs and motivations powered a new approach to campus engagement and professional certification.

Project Overview

Role

Academic Marketing Intern (UX + Community focus)

Duration

June 2024 – September 2024

Team

Marketing, University Outreach, Student Success

Tools

LinkedIn, Qualtrics, Office Suite, Canva, Adobe Suite, Hubspot

Methods

Design process, Stakeholder interviews, Student Surveys, Competitive Analysis, Iterative Testing, User Journey

The Challenge

Despite Teradata's robust certification programs offering valuable career credentials, university students faced significant barriers to participation. The existing system lacked student focused design, resulting in low awareness, poor completion rates, and missed opportunities for both students and Teradata's campus recruitment efforts.
The core user problem: Students wanted to advance their data analytics skills and career prospects but found certification programs overwhelming, unclear, and lacking the community support needed for sustained motivation.

Understanding The Users

I conducted comprehensive user research through surveys with Teradata summer intern cohort of 20+ students across target universities, in-depth interviews with 12 students at various certification stages, and stakeholder interviews at Teradata.



The Career Builder


Seeking to develop new skills, feels overwhelmed by the numerous motivated to start if they can find a clear certification pathway and will value recognition of their achievements.

The Community Learner


Hopes for a collaborative and peer-supported environment, they believe that engaging and learning with peers and industry professionals will give them success.

The Credential Collector


Prioritizes efficient learning paths and is motivated by visible achievements and recognition that will help them stand out to employers and demonstrate their commitment to a career in data.

User Journey Mapping

Through journey mapping exercises and survey analysis, three key barriers emerged:

Discovery and Clarity Gap


Students were unable to easily find or understand certification requirements and benefits.

Community Driven Dropout


Lack of peer community and structured guidance led to high dropout rates.

Value Proposition Doubt


Students questioned whether completing certifications would meaningfuly impact their career prospects.

Ideation & Solutions

Teradata Student Groups HUB


A centralized digital hub where students could discover certifications, track progress, access resources, and connect with peers in their local area and universities. Designed with intuitive navigation, progress visualization, and community features to address the clarity and support gaps.

Structured Learning Pathways


Visual certification roadmaps breaking down complex requirements into manageable steps, with clear timelines, prerequisites, and career outcome information. Each pathway included success stories from recent graduates in relevant roles.

Campus Ambassador Program


Peer led initiative training successful certification completers to mentor and support new cohorts. Ambassadors facilitated study groups, hosted information sessions, and created authentic student to student connections.

Guest Keynotes


Industry managers and experts share their knowledge, answer questions, and connect with students. These sessions motivate and inspire individuals by showing them what's possible with a Teradata certification.

Results & Impact

+2

pilot programs succesfully launched in targetted univeristies.

↑

Increase in student certification enrollments within first semester of implementation.

↓

Significant decrease in certification dropout rates compared to previous unstructured approach.

Key Learnings

Reflecting on this project, I realized that peer led community building, transparent progress tracking, and clear career messaging are far more effective than tradition methods for reducing certification dropout rates. Looking back, I feel I should have integrated the program more closely with university academic schedules and calendars while exploring more diverse partnerships to test its scalibility. This journey has taught me that community amplifies individual motivation and that transparency builds crucial trust with users. I learned that designing for a community requires a differernt perspective and approach than designing for individuals, and that sustainable engagement demands sytematic support. This project has deeply infomred my design practice, and I am excited to apply these community centered principles to my future initiatives that empower users through education, connection, and achievement.
