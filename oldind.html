Harnessing Inner and Outer Peace: IAHV and The Art of Living

By: Poorvi Hariharan

Aggression and violence are on the rise in today's world. Every day, we witness an alarming number of mass shootings, which indicate a fundamentally dangerous trend. Statistics show that two mass shootings occur in the United States every day, underscoring the urgent need for reform.

The International Association for Human Values (IAHV) is a global organization dedicated to promoting peace, resilience, and sustainable development through personal transformation and community empowerment. Founded by Sri Sri Ravi Shankar, IAHV offers programs that combine ancient wisdom with modern techniques, such as meditation and breathwork, to foster inner peace and well-being. The organization works across various sectors, including education, health, and conflict resolution, to address the root causes of violence and promote a harmonious society. Through its initiatives, IAHV aims to build a compassionate and peaceful world where every individual can thrive.

Meditation and breathwork, as advocated by Sri Sri Ravi Shankar, emerge as powerful strategies for dealing with this problem. We can achieve inner calm and empathy by shifting our attention within. These techniques enable us to address the underlying reasons for aggressiveness, offering healing to individuals who have been seriously hurt and stop the cycle of violence.
